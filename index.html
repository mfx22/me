Projects

 

Capacitance Meter

I designed a capacitance meter on PIC32 Microcontroller. Capacitance was calculated by measuring the time it takes for a capacitor to charge to a threshold voltage in which a hardware comparator was used as an interupt. Our capacitance meter measured within < 2% error and could handle a range from 1 to 100 nF.
 

Electric Longboard

Using funding from the Maker Club, I built a RC electric longboard from scratch that could reach speeds of 18 mph and has a range of about 20 miles. In this build, I learned about motors and speed controllers, lithium ion batteries, and radio control.
 

LED Matrix Arcade Game Suite

The LED Matrix arcade game suite includes several simple iconic games from our childhood made using a LED matrix and the FRDM-K64F microcontroller. These games included stacker, jump, and tetris. The suite includes a game selection interface and a two button control panel to select and play games.
 

Shazam Recreation

In this project, I recreated the popular music app, Shazam, which is able to recognize songs as you play them. In the project, I used concepts from FFTs on MATLAB to execute this. With an average recognition time of 5.14 seconds, our script was able to correctly identify songs from a library of 50 different tracks.
 

1 DoF PID Helicopter

A hovering drone motor was created that could hover at specified angles using PID control. This project was created on the PIC 32 microcontroller. The user could not only set the hover angle but could also adjust coefficients for the PID control. The motor angle was further outputted through a DAC output to be seen on an oscilloscope.
 

FireFingers

FireFingers is a device created for people to learn instruments with complex hand movements. In recording mode, a user can have the device memorize their hand movements. These data files can be shared over the internet. In playback mode, vibration motors on the joints of the hand indicate when you should move your fingers in order to perform the task or play a particular song. Instruments such as the piano, flute, or guitar can all benefit greatly from FireFingers.
 

AM Radio Transmitter

In this project, we worked to build an AM radio transmitter with very basic circuit components including FETS, resistors, inductors, and capacitors. The stages of the transmitter included a differential oscillator, a Gilbert cell mixer, and a power amplifier. We were able design the circuit and then transmit music across the room to a commercial receiver.
 

Maze Mapping Robot

This autonomous robot has a lasercut chassis and can follow lines and detect walls in order to fully navigate and map a 9 x 9 maze. There is a radio on board the robot that transmits the maze walls to an GUI on a different computer. In addition, a camera and FPGA is on board for image processing so that the robot can detect colors and shapes of what the camera sees.
 

Toast Printer

The toast printer is a device that can connect to the internet and "print" custom images on your toast using a heat gun. Operating much like a 3D printer, the device uses a PIC 32 and Raspberry Pi Zero W to retrieve images or check the weather to operate in different modes to print the weather, a custom note, or a cute picture on your bread.
 

SmartCharge

Smartcharge is a device that I pitched in an entrepreneurship competition, winning second place. SmartCharge is a replacement for any traditional outlet that can sense the current drawn from the outlet. When there is a rapid drop in current, it cuts off power completely in order to prevent trickle charging and save both energy and money.
 

Biodiesel Reactor

Along with the rest of the ESW Biofuels R&D team, I designed and built a biodiesel reactor for the community to use. We collect waste vegetable oil from various houses and local restaurants and use our reactor to turn this waste into usable biodiesel. This biodiesel is then gifted to the Cornell University Agricultural Research Station which uses it to power its mowers and tractors. This has been a large endeavor and though it doesn't involve any electrical work, it has been a continued passion of mine.
 

Algae Bioreactor

The algal CO2 scrubber project aimed to explore the growth of algae and develop a device to be used in homes with an effluent chimney. Algae in the scrubber would have access to sunlight and carbon dioxide for growth (and thus filter out the gas). The scubber includes a lasercut wooden base, acrylic housing tubes, two aquarium pump, as well as a colour sensor and temperature sensor to monitor growth. This algae would reach carrying capacity after 2 months, which would ideally be harvested for biodiesel conversion.
